Classification key

  • VERIFIED WITH EVIDENCE - sprint evidence present, safe to discuss
  • VERIFIED NO REFERENCE - add a sprint reference to strengthen claim
  • LEARNING TARGET - do not claim as verified
  • STUDYING - personal study only, not claim-ready
  • NO EVIDENCE - gap identified, do not claim
  • PUBLIC RISK - public visibility set but evidence incomplete
  • JD SIGNAL UNMATCHED - appears in JDs, not yet in your ledger
  • CLAIM SAFE - evidence confirmed, safe to discuss

Classifications are deterministic - derived from your Skill Ledger evidence_level and visibility fields. They are not AI-generated assessments.

Claim-safety examples

Claim-safety examples are advisory only.

Unsafe examples show wording to avoid.

Safer examples still require manual evidence review.

Do not use learning-target, studying, or no-evidence skills as verified claims.

A JD signal does not prove proficiency.

Manual review is required before using any claim in a CV, LinkedIn, public profile, cover letter, or interview answer.

This page does not update CVs, LinkedIn, public profiles, applications, or Skill Ledger evidence.

No live AI model is used in this advisory page.

These examples do not certify proficiency or predict employer outcomes.

Scenario: Learning-target skill claim inflation

Unsafe inflated claim: Expert in Databricks with production pipeline ownership.

Safer evidence-grounded alternative: Databricks is a learning target; portfolio evidence is planned but not yet verified.

Manual review note: Do not present a learning-target skill as verified until sprint evidence, tests, or work examples are reviewed manually.

Scenario: JD signal mistaken as proof of proficiency

Unsafe inflated claim: Proficient in Airflow because it appears in saved job descriptions.

Safer evidence-grounded alternative: Airflow appears as a JD signal only; gather or review evidence before claiming it.

Manual review note: A JD signal is advisory context, not proof that you already meet the requirement.

Scenario: Verified skill without enough public evidence context

Unsafe inflated claim: Python expert ready for any senior analytics role.

Safer evidence-grounded alternative: Python has private sprint evidence; describe the project scope only after manual review.

Manual review note: Even when evidence exists privately, public wording must match reviewed scope and limits.

Scenario: Public CV, LinkedIn, or profile wording before manual review

Unsafe inflated claim: Add Snowflake to LinkedIn as a core verified skill today.

Safer evidence-grounded alternative: Keep Snowflake in a private learning plan until public evidence is reviewed manually.

Manual review note: Review CV, LinkedIn, public profile, cover letter, and interview wording manually before publishing any claim.
