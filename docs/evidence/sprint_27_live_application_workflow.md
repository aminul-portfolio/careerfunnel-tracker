## Daily Live Application Workflow

1. Search suitable roles on job boards, company sites, or recruiter posts.
2. Apply the role-fit gate (see checklist below).
3. Save the job URL and full JD (paste into notes if needed).
4. Choose the correct Application Pack document (see selection table).
5. Attach or use `Aminul_Islam_Data_Analyst_CV` (do not rename for normal applications).
6. Draft or tailor the cover letter using Steps 1-3.
7. Run the claim-safety gate before sending.
8. Submit the application manually on the employer portal or by email.
9. Record the application in CareerFunnel Tracker.
10. Save evidence notes (JD, variant used, projects mentioned, claim-safety result).
11. Plan follow-up (3-day or 7-day message from Step 4 if appropriate).

## Role-Fit Gate Before Applying

- [ ] Role title matches target direction (Data, BI, Reporting, FinTech analytics, Operations, Commercial).
- [ ] Location/work mode works from Purley/London/UK remote.
- [ ] Seniority is realistic (not senior platform lead unless stretch and worth it).
- [ ] Must-have tools are honestly covered (Python, SQL skill, Excel, Django portfolio where relevant).
- [ ] No hard blocker such as mandatory Power BI/Tableau artefact requirement you cannot evidence.
- [ ] No hard blocker such as advanced cloud/dbt/Airflow/Spark/Kafka unless JD is stretch and you accept skip or honest stretch apply.
- [ ] No sponsorship issue (UK ILR; no sponsorship required).
- [ ] No scam or vague company risk (verify company name and posting source).
- [ ] Role is worth either apply, stretch apply, or skip.

## Cover Letter Workflow

1. Use Step 3 when full JD is available (paste JD, extract requirements, map evidence).
2. Use Step 2 when the role type is obvious (Data Analyst, BI/Reporting, FinTech, Operations/Commercial).
3. Use Step 1 for general/fallback wording when the posting is simple or time is limited.
4. Mention one domain evidence block only (financial operations, KPI reporting, or portfolio as fits the role).
5. Mention one or two projects maximum (BakeOps Intelligence, CareerFunnel Tracker, TradeIntel 360, DataBridge Market API).
6. Keep under one page.
7. Attach or use `Aminul_Islam_Data_Analyst_CV`.

## Recruiter Outreach Workflow

1. Use Step 4 (`recruiter_outreach_message_variants.md`).
2. Keep first message under 140 words.
3. Lead with role fit and target role type.
4. Mention one domain strength (FX/reconciliation, KPI reporting, or portfolio discipline).
5. Mention one or two projects only if useful for the conversation.
6. Mention UK ILR/no sponsorship where useful.
7. Do not say LinkedIn profile has been updated (postponed until after Sprint 30).
8. Record message sent in CareerFunnel Tracker or application notes.

Keep targets realistic. Consistency and claim safety matter more than volume.

## Weekly Review Workflow

- [ ] Count applications submitted.
- [ ] Count responses received (recruiter reply, interview invite, rejection).
- [ ] Count rejections and note common reasons if stated.
- [ ] Review skipped roles (why skipped: blocker, seniority, location, scam risk).
- [ ] Review most common requirements in saved JDs (tools, domain, seniority).
- [ ] Update search keywords and boards based on fit patterns.
- [ ] Identify missing skill evidence (do not invent; note for future sprint or honest stretch only).
- [ ] Choose next week's role focus (e.g. Data Analyst vs FinTech analytics).
- [ ] Do not update LinkedIn until after Sprint 30.

## Application Quality Levels

| Level | Meaning | Use When |
|---|---|---|
| Quick Apply | Step 2 variant or Step 1 fallback; minimal tailoring; locked CV | Strong fit junior/mid Data or Operations role; simple portal apply |
| Standard Apply | Step 3 intake + Step 2 variant; full claim-safety and tracker record | Normal target role with full JD available |
| High-Value Apply | Step 3 full mapping; best project pair; optional recruiter outreach | Strong company, strong fit, worth extra tailoring time |
| Stretch Apply | Honest stretch wording; Junior Data Engineer or Analytics Engineer only if JD allows | JD is stretch but not blocked; accept lower response rate |
| Skip | Do not apply; record skip reason in notes | Blockers, scam risk, unsupported must-haves, or poor fit |
